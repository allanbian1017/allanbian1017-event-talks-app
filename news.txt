Latest News for June 16, 2026

1. INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS: U.S.-IRAN PEACE DEAL DEVELOPMENTS
Following a historic deal to end the conflict between the United States and Iran, President Donald Trump announced that the strategic Strait of Hormuz will be "completely open" and toll-free for all international shipping. While the U.S. has indicated a potential reconstruction fund for Iran, officials emphasized that the release of funds and economic benefits will be strictly "tied to performance". In addition, President Trump stated that Iran has agreed to never pursue nuclear weapons, and Vice President JD Vance confirmed that nuclear inspectors will be allowed to return to Iran as part of the agreement. However, Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi expressed caution, citing a history of broken U.S. commitments.

2. BUSINESS AND ECONOMY: FORTUNE SOUTHEAST ASIA 500
Fortune released its 2026 rankings of the top companies in Southeast Asia. The Singapore-headquartered commodity trader Trafigura Group topped the list for the third consecutive year, reporting a massive $240.3 billion in revenue. Singapore-based companies continue to dominate the top tier, holding three of the top five spots. Meanwhile, Vietnam emerged as a major growth story, with its companies seeing an aggregate revenue increase of 10.5%, which is triple the regional average.
